Magnetic Motion Demo

Magnetic Motion Demo is a simple demonstration of physics (mostly momentum and acceleration) created in Java. Originally created as a math project, this demo is operated exclusively through the mouse. A left click will shoot the ball from the launcher. A right click will spawn a negative electric charge. This electric charge will deflect the ball. An additional right click will change the negative charge into a positive one, which will attract the ball. Clicking the charge once more will delete it. The middle mouse button will spawn a bumper, which can interact with the charges the same way the ball does. It will also rebound with balls, simulating the act of transfer of momentum. The two facets of magnetic attraction and momentum should serve as a fun physics demo. A jar is available for easy access to the demo!

How and Why Magnetic Motion Demo Was Made

The Magnetic Motion Demo was originally created as my final project for Calculus AB. However, functionally it served as my first foray into the world of Libgdx and the many advantages it offers. Because of the integration of the Libgdx framework, this project took me roughly two weeks, starting with my education of how to work with Libgdx and ending with the actual implementation of the code. Though a small project, it is a rather cool demonstration of physics (if I do say so myself), and can be useful as a learning tool for those who have just been introduced to Libgdx. NOTE: As this was my first project in Libgdx, the coding (especially the methods for rendering) is a bit raw and unoptimized. Regardless, it is easily read and comprehensible.

Language-wise, the Magnetic Motion Demo is completely written in Java, and is built upon the Libgdx Java engine. However, Libgdx is mostly used for the rendering of the sprites and the background, and thus is largely unnecessary to understand the code of this game. The game can be understood with a beginner's grasp of Java, and can be used as a learning tool.
